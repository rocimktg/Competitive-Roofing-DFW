Repairs We Handle Every Day

Most roofing problems in DFW come from the same handful of causes. We've seen and fixed all of them — and we know which ones are quick fixes and which ones are warning signs of something bigger. We'll tell you honestly which category you're in.

Active Leaks & Water Intrusion

If you're seeing water stains on your ceiling, finding wet insulation in your attic, or noticing moisture around a chimney or skylight after rain, you have an active intrusion point that needs to be located and closed before the next storm. Leaks are almost never where you think they are — water travels along decking, rafters, and insulation before it appears inside. We trace the source from the roof down, find the actual entry point, and repair it correctly. Tarping and caulk are temporary measures; we do the actual fix.

Missing, Cracked, or Lifted Shingles

DFW wind events — even ones that don't qualify as major storms — can pull up tab shingles, crack brittle shingles in older sections, or lift entire sections of roofing in severe cases. A single missing shingle exposes felt and decking to direct UV and rain. We carry a broad stock of shingle colors and profiles so we can match your existing roof closely. When an exact match isn't possible, we'll tell you upfront and discuss your options rather than installing something that looks wrong and affects your home's appearance.

Flashing Failures Around Chimneys, Vents & Valleys

Flashing is the metal sheeting that seals the transitions between your roof surface and any penetration — chimneys, pipes, skylights, dormers, and the valleys where two roof planes meet. It's one of the most common failure points on older roofs, and it's frequently installed incorrectly on newer ones. Failed flashing can leak for years before you notice interior damage. We remove and replace damaged flashing rather than layering caulk over it — the caulk-over approach fails within a year or two and leaves the underlying problem intact.

Hail & Storm Damage

Texas hail can range from minor granule loss — which shortens shingle life but doesn't cause immediate leaks — to severe impact damage that cracks shingles, dents metal flashing, and compromises the roof's structural integrity in affected areas. We document hail damage with photos and written scope, which is exactly what your insurance adjuster needs to process a claim. If the damage is limited to a portion of the roof, we can sometimes do a targeted repair rather than a full replacement. We'll tell you which is the right call based on the actual condition of the roof, not what's more profitable for us.

Ventilation & Ridge Issues

Inadequate attic ventilation is one of the most underdiagnosed roofing problems in DFW. When hot air can't exit the attic, it superheats the decking and accelerates shingle deterioration — sometimes by years. It also creates moisture traps in winter that lead to decking rot and mold growth. If your energy bills have crept up or your shingles are aging faster than they should, ventilation is often the culprit. We assess intake and exhaust airflow and can add ridge vents, soffit vents, or box vents as needed to bring your attic ventilation into balance.

Gutter & Fascia Damage

Gutters and fascia boards are closely tied to roof performance — overflowing or improperly pitched gutters can back water up under your drip edge, which eventually leads to fascia rot and soffit damage. Sagging gutters, separated gutter seams, and damaged downspouts all cause water to deposit against your foundation and fascia instead of away from the structure. We repair and reseat gutters as part of roof repair work when the two are connected, rather than treating them as separate unrelated issues.

Repair or Replace? Here's How We Think About It.

We get this question on almost every repair call. The honest answer depends on the age of the roof, the extent of the damage, and whether the rest of the roof is in good shape. Here's the framework we use — and we'll walk through it with you on your specific roof.

Repair is usually the right call when…

  • Roof is under 15 years old and in otherwise good condition
  • Damage is isolated to one section or a specific penetration point
  • The underlying decking is solid with no soft spots
  • No evidence of widespread granule loss or shingle brittleness
  • A matching shingle is available for the affected area
  • Insurance is covering a specific storm event, not general wear

Replacement often makes more sense when…

  • Roof is 20+ years old and showing widespread wear
  • Multiple areas need repair simultaneously
  • Decking damage is found under multiple sections
  • A third or more of the roof surface is affected by storm damage
  • Previous repairs have already been done to the same areas
  • Insurance is covering a full replacement — repair cost approaches replacement

Storm Damage & Insurance-Covered Repairs

Not every storm event triggers a full replacement — sometimes the damage is limited and a targeted repair is the right scope. But even for repairs, the insurance process matters. Here's what you should know.

Texas homeowner's insurance covers storm-related damage to your roof — wind, hail, and falling objects are the most common triggers. The challenge is that adjusters are often looking at roofs from the ground or doing quick drive-bys to assess damage volume, and they frequently miss localized damage to flashing, valleys, and pipe boots. These are exactly the areas where small failures turn into big leaks.

When Craig does your inspection, he documents every item with photos and a written scope. If you file a claim, we're available to meet the adjuster on-site and walk the roof together. This single step — having a contractor on the roof during the adjuster visit — is the biggest factor in whether your claim covers the full scope of the damage. Adjusters miss things. We catch them.

What we document

  • Impact marks and granule loss from hail — with measurement
  • Lifted, cracked, or missing shingles from wind
  • Flashing damage and displacement at penetration points
  • Damaged gutters, drip edge, and fascia
  • Any interior water damage traceable to the roof event
  • Ventilation issues exacerbated by storm impact

What adjusters commonly miss

  • Pipe boot and vent flashing damage (not visible from ground)
  • Valley damage in interior roof sections
  • Granule loss severity on north-facing slopes
  • Drip edge displacement under gutter lines
  • Soft spots indicating underlayment saturation
  • Partial ridge cap damage from wind uplift

Common Questions About Roof Repairs

How quickly can you respond to an active leak?

For active water intrusion, we prioritize same-day response. Call Craig directly at (817) 454-2113. If you've got water coming in during or after a storm, the first step is stopping the damage — we can deploy temporary protection while we assess the permanent fix. Don't wait through a second or third rain event hoping it seals itself. It won't.

Can you match my existing shingles?

In most cases, yes — we carry a wide range of shingle profiles, colors, and manufacturers. Older roofs can be harder to match, especially if the shingle line has been discontinued. When we do the estimate, we'll tell you exactly how close the match will be before any work begins. If you're concerned about aesthetics, we can discuss options for the affected section that blend better with the surrounding surface.

Will a repair void my manufacturer's warranty?

It depends on the warranty terms and who does the work. Most manufacturer warranties require repairs to be performed by a licensed contractor using approved materials. We qualify under both requirements. If you have a specific warranty document you're concerned about, send it to us and we'll review it before we start — we'd rather know about a warranty restriction upfront than find out about it later.

What's the difference between a repair and a patch?

A patch is a temporary fix — typically a piece of flashing, a tube of roofing cement, or a piece of mismatched shingle applied over a problem area. Patches are what other contractors do when they're trying to get in and out quickly. A proper repair means removing the compromised material, inspecting what's underneath, replacing the underlayment if needed, and installing new material correctly using the same methods and fasteners as the original installation. We do repairs, not patches.

Should I file an insurance claim for my repair?

It depends on the cause of the damage and your deductible. If the damage is from a covered weather event — hail, wind, a falling branch — it's worth at least getting an inspection to document the scope before you decide. If the repair cost is close to or below your deductible, filing a claim may not be worth it. We'll give you an honest assessment of what the repair will cost, what the damage scope looks like, and whether it makes sense to involve your insurance company. We don't push claims — we just tell you the facts.
