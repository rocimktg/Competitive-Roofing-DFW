What's Included in Every Roof Replacement

We don't offer tiered packages or bait-and-switch upgrades. Every replacement we do includes these components as standard — because skimping on any of them means callbacks down the road, and we're not interested in that.

Owens Corning Shingles

We install Owens Corning shingles on every project unless the customer specifically requests otherwise. They carry a strong manufacturer warranty, impact resistance built for North Texas hail seasons, and wind performance that significantly outperforms cheaper alternatives. We've been installing them long enough to know how they hold up in DFW summers and spring hail seasons — and they hold up well. Owens Corning manufactures shingles at facilities right here in North Texas, so when we order your materials, they're not shipping cross-country. If you've been quoted on a cheaper shingle elsewhere, ask the other contractor what their callback rate looks like after the first year.

Synthetic Underlayment

The underlayment is the last line of defense between your decking and the elements — it matters a lot more than most homeowners realize. We use synthetic underlayment on every job instead of traditional felt. It's lighter, more tear-resistant, and doesn't absorb moisture the way 15- or 30-pound felt does. If a shingle gets blown off in a storm before the insurance crew shows up, your underlayment is what keeps water out of your attic. We don't cut corners there.

Ridge Cap Shingles & Proper Ventilation

We install purpose-built ridge cap shingles on every job — not cut-down shingles used as a cheaper substitute. We also assess attic ventilation on every replacement. Poor ventilation shortens shingle life and drives up cooling costs, and it's something a lot of contractors don't bother checking. If we find an issue during tear-off, we'll tell you what we found and what it costs to address — before we close everything back up.

Full Tear-Off & Debris Removal

We tear off all existing roofing material down to the decking before anything new goes on. Some contractors will reroof over an existing layer to save time — this adds weight to your structure, traps heat and moisture between layers, and voids most manufacturer warranties. We don't do that. We also inspect the decking for soft spots, rot, or damaged sheathing while we're down to bare wood, and we replace what needs replacing. Your yard gets cleaned up the same day we finish. No piles of shingles left in your driveway, no stray nails in the grass.

Common Questions

How long does a full roof replacement take?

Most residential replacements in DFW are completed in a single day. Larger homes, complex rooflines, or jobs that require significant decking repair may run into a second day. We don't start a job we can't close properly — you won't have a half-finished roof sitting overnight unless there's a genuine weather delay. We communicate before, during, and after.

Do I need to be home during the replacement?

You don't need to be home during the work, but we recommend being available by phone. Craig will do a walkthrough with you at the end of the job to review the finished roof and go over any decking issues we found, ventilation notes, or warranty paperwork. If you can't be there, we'll document everything and go over it with you remotely — but most customers find it worth being around for the final walkthrough.

What happens if you find rotten or damaged decking?

We inspect the decking during tear-off and document anything that needs attention with photos. If we find soft spots or damaged sheathing, we'll contact you immediately with the square footage, the cost to replace it, and photos so you can see exactly what we're looking at. We don't proceed without your approval. Decking replacement is priced per sheet and is typically a small addition to the overall job cost.

Will my homeowner's insurance cover a full replacement?

If the damage is from a covered event — hail, wind, a fallen tree — most standard Texas homeowner's policies will cover the replacement minus your deductible. The key is having an advocate on the roof during the adjuster visit. We've seen adjusters miss flashing damage, gutter damage, and underlayment issues that are legitimately covered. When Craig is on the roof during that inspection, those items get documented and included in the claim. We also file supplemental claims when the initial payout doesn't reflect the actual scope.

Do you pull permits?

Yes — where required. Coppell, Southlake, and several other DFW cities require permits for full roof replacements. We register with the city, pull the permit, and schedule any required inspections. Some contractors skip this step to save time and money. We don't. A roof installed without a required permit can create issues when you sell the home, void your homeowner's insurance coverage, or lead to fines if the city finds out during a neighbor's inspection.
